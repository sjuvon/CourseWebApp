(Mon 02/11) First types of morphism
Class 15 Open subschemes and immersions; extending sheaves by zero; closed subschemes and immersions; open and closed immersions satisfy the good properties of morphisms; the reduced induced closed subscheme structure. (Wed 02/13) Open and closed immersions

Class 17 Constructing \( \mathrm{Proj}(S) \) for a graded ring \( S \), following EGA II; \( \mathbb{P}^n_R \) as \( \mathrm{Proj} \ R[x_0,\ldots,x_n] \). (Mon 02/18) \( \mathrm{Proj}(S) \)
Class 18 Locally closed immersions; the graph of a morphism; separated schemes and morphisms; the locus where two morphisms agree. (Wed 02/20) Separated schemes and morphisms
Class 19 Universally closed morphisms; proper morphisms; proper morphisms satisfy the good properties; closed immersions are proper, the affine line is not; \( \mathbb{P}^n_R \) is proper over \( \mathrm{Spec}(R) \), a.k.a. the main theorem of elimination theory; classical examples of the main theorem; criterion for separatedness; Grothendieck's proof of the main theorem. (Fri 02/22) Proper morphisms

Class 20 Grothendieck's proof that \( \mathbb{P}^n_R \rightarrow \mathrm{Spec}(R) \) is proper, done right; valuation rings; Spec of a discrete valuation ring; the valuative criterion for noetherian schemes; an application of the valuative criterion: the affine line with doubled origin; the valuative criterion as found in EGA. (Mon 02/25) Valuative criteria
Class 21 \( \mathscr{O}_X \)-modules of a ringed space \( (X, \mathscr{O}_X) \) form an abelian category; the sheaf \( \widetilde{M} \) of an affine scheme \( \mathrm{Spec}(R) \); the assignment \( M \mapsto \widetilde{M} \) defines an exact equivalence between the category of \( R \)-modules and the category of \( \mathscr{O}_X \)-modules of the form \( \widetilde{M} \). (Wed 02/27) \( \widetilde{M} \)
Class 22 Quasicoherent sheaves on a scheme \( X \); quasicoherence is affine-local: it suffices to verify it on some open affine cover of \( X \). (Fri 03/01) Quasicoherent sheaves

Class 23 Quasicoherent sheaves form an abelian category \( \mathrm{\mathbf{QCoh}}(X) \); ideal sheaves are precisely the quasicoherent sheaves of ideals; the tensor product \( F \otimes_{\mathscr{O}_X} G\) of quasicoherent sheaves is quasicoherent; tensor powers of quasicoherent sheaves \( T^n(F) \), \( \mathrm{Sym}^n(F) \), \( \bigwedge\nolimits^n(F) \); free and locally free sheaves; quasicoherent sheaves are presented by locally free sheaves; finite properties of quasicoherent sheaves: finite type, finitely presented, coherent; coherent sheaves form an abelian category \( \mathrm{\mathbf{Coh}}(X) \). (Mon 03/04) Examples of quasicoherent sheaves
Class 24 Finite type sheaves, finitely presented sheaves, coherent sheaves; tensor product of coherent sheaves; sheaf hom \( \mathrm{\underline{Hom}}_X(F,G) \); locally free sheaves of finite rank; vector bundles and locally free sheaves. (Wed 03/06) Sheaves of modules continued
